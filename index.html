A Journey Into a New Technology Frontier

The journey of Patrick McGill revolved a three defined skills hardned through the small yet meaningful years of software developemnt and software engineering work.

These are the following skills learned:


Dedication

A developer should be dedicated to the work they are developing. Whether it be a passion project, a commissioned work, or for a job, the developer should be dedicated to the project they are working on. My first years as a developer taught me to dedicate myself to anything put infront of me. I started out with no knowledge in the development world. The first week at the Eleven Fifty Academy forced me out of my comfort zone and knew immediately that dedication was needed to advance through the Python course. Through out of course lesson through third party means or asking for help from instructors, the means to pass through the course and begin the journey into the scary, yet exciting new frontier.

Dedication had always been a part of my life. From traveling to different cities playing soccer while growing up to playing games at a competitive levels to the point of attending tournaments, dedication was needed to get better blocker brought to me. Without any sort of dedication, advancement in ones goals will be haltered. I embraced any blocker brought to me and was dedicated to get past the hurdles ahead.


Adaptabilty

Adaptability is one of the most difficult skills to learn. New tasks, new software, new coding frameworks, new coworkers, and new goals are events that a person comes across througout their life. Whether in the business world or ones personal life, adaptability is one constant needed to advance. Early life had taught me to adapt. New opponents in sports and games, new strategies that needed to be learned, a surprise development in a task assigned to me. Adaptablity helped me with all of this. My very first coding language was HTML5 and CSS. This was to learn the very basic levels of coding for Front End Development. Then Python was obviously brought into my repertoire through the Eleven Fifty academy. This was a new blocker into the new journey I was taking. Python came natural to me and being able to relate the knowledge gained first in HTML5 and CSS helped with grasping Python quickly. The course brought me farther in my advancement in development than I would have ever expected. Then a job offer came in after attending the academy. A new path to take. This path brought many hurdles to come over: two new coding languages I was not familiar with, a fresh environment with a new dynamic along with new colleagues, and a whole new way to present myself. To succeed, only one thing was needed to be done. Adapt.


Adventurous

Life without adventure becomes very stagnant. Have you ever eaten at the same restuarant day in and day out while having the same meal? It becomes bland and you just flow through the motions of eating that meal. This goes the same for life. Being adventurous has a plehtora of advantages when being a developer and a human. Learning new coding languages and frameworks goes hand in hand with being adventurous. This skill is one needed to grow in life and one taught fast. While a routine is a good thing to have, breaking the routine is needed. I had choices to stay where I was before I started coding and I took a huge chance and took the jump into the crazy and every changing world of development. This has become one of my most powerful skills learned.
